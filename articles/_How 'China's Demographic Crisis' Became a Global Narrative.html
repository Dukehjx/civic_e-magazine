The Politics of Population: How 'China's Demographic Crisis' Became a Global Narrative 人口政治学："中国人口危机"如何成为全球叙事

Demography • January 2026 人口学 • 2026年1月

Western narratives about China's population crisis have intensified their public opinion offensive. This article analyzes how the West constructs the "China population crisis theory" through media and think tanks, and the role of this narrative in international politics.

I. Western Assessments of China's Population Crisis

1. Population Scale: Promoting "Population Collapse Theory"

Western media and think tanks began promoting the "China faces population crisis" theory starting in 2021, even before China's seventh census results were published. The UK's Financial Times heavily promoted that China's population may have already fallen below 1.4 billion in 2021, marking the first decline since the 1950s. In 2023, the U.S. Census Bureau released a report predicting China's population will peak in 2031 and then gradually decline.

2. Total Fertility Rate: Predicted to Remain Below 1.5 Long-Term

In July 2023, the U.S. Census Bureau released a report predicting China's total fertility rate would be only 1.18 in 2023. The Brookings Institution research report predicted China's total fertility rate will remain below 1.3 long-term. The Economist published articles predicting China's total fertility rate will remain below 1.5 long-term.

3. Aging Level: Promoting Aging Speed Surpassing Developed Countries

The European Commission Joint Research Centre (JRC) predicts that by 2050, over 26% of China's population (more than 350 million people) will be 65 or older. The Lancet research predicts that by 2100, 35% of China's population will be 65 or older. Australia's Lowy Institute analysis suggests that due to population aging, China's workforce will decrease by 42 million by 2030.

II. Western Public Opinion Offensive Situation

1. Using "Population Collapse Theory" to Undermine China's Development Potential and Comparative Advantages

The West attempts to magnify China's slowing population growth—a phased issue—and undermine future Chinese economic growth potential. For example, The Economist claims China's negative population growth is "irreversible" and will lead to long-term decline in economic growth. Additionally, the West attempts to weaken foreign investment enthusiasm for China by promoting the population crisis narrative. For example, The New York Times links China's population decrease to "failure of the China model," predicting labor shortages will cause manufacturing outflow, suggesting "the China rise myth will end."

2. Promoting "Aging Society Crisis" to Undermine China's Consumer Market and Tech Competitiveness

First, using aging society crisis to undermine China's consumer market vitality. The West hypes risks in China's pension system, suggesting domestic consumer spending power will rapidly shrink. Second, undermining China's tech industry international competitiveness. Linking population aging issues with China's frontier technology and industrial development, claiming aging will suppress tech investment, causing China to fall behind the U.S. in frontier fields like AI.

3. Linking China's Population Issues with Geopolitics to Create "China Threat" Narrative

Foreign Policy articles speculate China may divert domestic contradictions through military adventures, promoting Taiwan Strait and South China Sea tensions. The Washington Post links population issues to political systems, claiming "authoritarian models cannot address aging challenges." RAND Corporation reports predict China may engage in military strategic adventures during population contraction periods.

III. Countermeasures and Recommendations

1. Deconstruct Western Narrative Logic Fragmentation, Precisely Counter Western Pessimistic Tunes

First, accelerate compilation and public release of "China Population High-Quality Development White Paper," objectively presenting China's population development status and related policy achievements. Emphasize indicators like "China's working-age population (16-59)," "resident population urbanization rate," and "population education levels" to demonstrate structural population advantages. Second, use "Belt and Road" and "Global South" cooperation mechanisms to strengthen international publicity and compete for international discourse power.

2. Target U.S. Domestic Population Problem Vulnerabilities, Expose False Narratives Through Offense as Defense

The United States also faces severe domestic population problems. Pew Research Center shows the proportion of American adults under 50 without children rose from 37% in 2018 to 47% in 2023. Current Trump administration policies of protecting U.S. borders through hardline measures, reducing illegal immigration, and being extremely unfriendly to Chinese students conflict with U.S. population growth paths. China should promptly speak out and point out these contradictions.

3. Continuously Optimize Population Development Strategy to Promote China's High-Quality Population Development

First, clarify the logical relationship between birth incentive policies and delayed retirement policies. Recommend changing the "one-size-fits-all" delayed retirement policy to allow early retirement for those who will retire and have children. Second, precisely optimize "elderly and young" services, develop the "silver economy," while considering expanding service industry opening to the outside. Third, lower international technical talent immigration thresholds and accelerate formulation of timetables and task lists for attracting immigrants.

美西方炮制中国人口危机强化对华舆论新攻势。本文分析了美西方如何通过媒体和智库构建"中国人口危机论"，以及这一叙事在国际政治中的作用。

一、美西方对我人口危机的评估

1、人口规模方面：渲染"人口坍塌论"

美西方国家媒体和智库研究机构炮制"中国面临人口危机论"发端于2021年，即中国"七普"结果公布之前，英国《金融时报》大肆渲染中国在2021年人口已不足14亿人，自上世纪50年代以来首次下降。2023年，美国人口普查局（U.S. Census Bureau）发布报告预测中国人口将在2031年达到峰值，之后逐步下降。

2、总和生育率方面：预计长期低于1.5

2023年7月，美国人口普查局发布报告预测2023年中国总和生育率仅为1.18；布鲁金斯学会研究报告预测中国总和生育率将长期低于1.3；《经济学人》发表文章预测中国总和生育率将长期低于1.5。

3、老龄化水平方面：渲染老龄化速度超越发达国家

欧盟委员会联合研究中心（JRC）预测中国2050年65岁以上人口占比超过26%（超过3.5亿人）；《柳叶刀》研究预测中国65岁以上人口将在2100年占比达35%；澳大利亚洛伊研究所分析认为，由于人口老龄化，中国在2030年的劳动力将减少4200万人。

二、美西方对我舆论攻势的情况梳理

1、借"人口坍塌论"唱衰中国发展潜力与比较优势

美西方试图将中国人口增速放缓这一阶段性问题放大，唱衰未来中国经济增长潜力。例如《经济学人》称中国人口负增长"不可逆转"，将导致经济增速长期下滑。其次，美西方试图通过渲染中国人口危机，削弱外资对华投资积极性。例如《纽约时报》将中国人口减少与"中国模式失败"挂钩，预测劳动力短缺将引发制造业外流，暗示"中国崛起神话将终结"。

2、渲染"老龄化社会危机"，唱衰中国消费市场与科技竞争力

一是借老龄化社会危机唱衰中国消费市场活力。西方通过炒作中国养老金体系风险，暗示国内消费者消费能力将快速萎缩。二是唱衰中国科技产业国际竞争力。将人口老龄化问题与中国前沿科技和产业发展捆绑炒作，认为老龄化将抑制科技投入，导致中国在AI等前沿领域被美国甩开。

3、将中国人口问题与地缘政治关联，制造"中国威胁"叙事

《外交政策》文章猜测中国可能通过军事冒险转移国内矛盾，渲染台海、南海紧张。《华盛顿邮报》则将人口问题与政治体制挂钩，称"威权模式无法应对老龄化挑战"。兰德公司报告预测人口收缩期中国可能会进行军事战略冒险。

三、对策建议

1、拆解美西方舆论逻辑碎片性，精准冲击美西方唱衰调

一是加快编制和公开发布《中国人口高质量发展白皮书》，客观呈现中国人口发展现状与相关政策成效。重点强调"中国16岁至59岁劳动年龄人口"、"常住人口城镇化率"、"人口受教育程度"等指标，展示人口结构性优势。二是利用"一带一路"、"全球南方"合作机制强化国际宣传，争夺国际话语权。

2、瞄准美国国内人口问题软肋，以攻代守拆穿虚假叙事

美国国内同样面临严峻人口问题。皮尤研究中心显示美国50岁以下没有孩子的成年人比例从2018年的37%上升至2023年的47%。当前特朗普政府通过强硬手段保护美国边境、减少非法移民数量、对中国留学生极其不友好等政策，与美国人口增长路径相冲突，我国应及时发声指出其矛盾之处。

3、持续优化人口发展战略，推动我国人口高质量发展

一是理顺生育激励政策与延迟退休政策逻辑关系。建议改变"一刀切"的延退政策，对育有子女的拟退休人员允许提前退休。二是精准优化"一老一小"服务，发展"银发经济"，同时考虑扩大服务业对外开放。三是降低国际技术人才移民门槛，加快制定吸引移民的时间表和任务清单。
