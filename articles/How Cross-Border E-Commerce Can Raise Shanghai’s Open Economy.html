How Cross-Border E-Commerce Can Raise Shanghai’s Open Economy

发挥跨境电商平台赋能效应

提升上海开放型经济能级的政策建议

跨境电商已经成为拉动外贸和产业出海的新动能，也是上海融入国际产业体系和提升开放驱动力的重要抓手。但是，上海在发挥跨境电商平台赋能效应，提升上海开放型经济能级方面仍然存在跨境电商产业规模能级不足、航空物流运力不足、平台外贸采购和进口便利化水平不足、传统贸易企业开展跨境电商业务积极性不足等瓶颈问题，本专报在系统梳理解剖上述问题基础上，提出上海破解跨境电商发展瓶颈，助推开放型经济高质量发展的政策建议

  1. 主要问题

1、跨境电商企业集聚度不足，与深圳差距持续拉大

上海凭借口岸资源优势，已经成为全国跨境电商平台的重要集聚地之一，且跨境金融服务体系完备，总体位居全国跨境电商产业第一梯队。但是，截至2024年7月，上海共计拥有2161家跨境电商企业，这与深圳的6983家存在显著差距。上海跨境电商交易平台共计611个，而深圳则有2402个。2023年上海跨境电商进出口额为2623.9亿元，同比增长42%；而深圳则为32265.3亿元，同比增速高达74.4%。总体来看，上海跨境电商无论是在企业数量规模，还是跨境电商交易额，其与深圳的差距均十分显著且具有持续拉大趋势。

2、空港航空运力遇天花板，海外仓建设亟待提速

2023年上海空港口岸出口跨境电商申报量达4.4亿票，同比增长近2倍，创历史新高；出口总值超850亿元人民币。空港的航空运力已经接近天花板水平。上海本土跨境电商龙头企业拼多多的负责人向课题组反映，目前虹桥机场航空运力已经无法跟上拼多多跨境电商业务增速需求，后续如何开辟更多航空货运渠道，以及如何进一步充分空海协同和联运等，以有效缓解航空运力不足困境，是企业和政府需要共同谋划的重要课题。

3、跨境电商外贸采购和进口便利化存在制度性障碍

一是目前正在施行的“跨境电子商务零售进口商品清单”相对受限。部分冷冻类、预制菜类等产品未进入海关正面清单，限制盒马等贸易平台发挥流量规模效应，不利于其提升内外贸一体化能力。二是重点领域面临制度约束过紧、审批制度与贸易需求错位等障碍。例如，作为进博会最重要展品之一，微型医疗器械产品的“展品变商品”注册审批流程漫长，审批流程亟待优化。三是时尚消费品的销售实时性特点对进口便利化具有更高要求。例如，化妆品按照一般贸易清关，其功能证明和检测的流程需要一年到两年，而化妆品“流行色”往往具有很强的应季性和时效性，这就造成国际化妆品公司难以在国内外同时首发新产品。同时，跨境电商零售进口商品单次交易不能超过5000元，无法开展大规模进口和销售。

4、传统贸易企业开展跨境电商业务积极性有待提升

课题组通过走访调研跨境公服、拼多多等跨境电商平台，发现目前上海传统贸易企业处于对资金成本、市场风险等因素的考量，对借助跨境电商平台实现自身产品和服务出海，很多持怀疑和观望态度，开展跨境电商业务积极性不足。已经开展跨境电商业务的贸易企业在出海过程中则遭遇到海外知识产权侵权、对海外政策法律与文化消费习俗不熟悉、支付结算与售后服务体系打造有困难等问题，削弱贸易企业跨境出海积极性。拼多多和跨境公服等企业负责人反映，目前开展跨境电商业务的贸易企业主要开展2C端业务，然而，2B端业务的跨境出海也应当被高度重视，依托跨境电商开展2B业务具备极大市场发展潜力。

二、政策建议

1、招引与培育相结合，壮大上海跨境电商产业生态能级

一是发挥龙头平台企业功能，开展大力度链式招商。充分发挥拼多多、携程等上海本土龙头跨境电商平台企业创新引领和生态主导功能，发挥平台业务链接优势，吸引上下游业务关联企业落户上海，政府相关主管部门制定基于上海本土跨境电商平台企业招引集聚企业落户的产业支持政策并做好楼宇、空间和服务等要素保障工作。二是建议由政府牵头编制完善跨境电商领域及细分赛道产业链图谱。以此为基准，精准对接世界500强、中国500强、央企国企、知名跨国公司等关联龙头企业,谋划生成一批跨境电商全产业链招商引资项目。实施全球招商合作伙伴计划，加强与龙头企业、国内外中介机构、产业基金、异地商会等招商合作。三是营造直播经济全产业生态，塑造上海跨境出海差异化比较优势。以上海市直播电商基地创建为抓手，引导企业发展“丝路电商”直播经济，打造直播样板间，探索直播赋能实体经济。动员上海本地高校联合拼多多、百秋、飞书深诺等平台企业，“从零到一”培养拼多多系、百秋系平台生态的主播、运营、短视频、营销策划等专业人才，孵化直播创客团队和短视频创客团队，入驻上海各大平台直播运营中心开展直播与短视频带货。

2、加快海外仓建设，增强跨境电商物流保障能力

一是统筹用好现有资金渠道，支持跨境电商海外仓企业发展。发挥服务业发展引导资金作用，引导更多社会资本以市场化方式支持跨境电商海外仓等相关企业发展。二是用好长宁现有股权投资基金资源，探索以市场化方式设立产业发展基金，加强对跨境电商海外仓企业支持。三是加快编制出台跨境电商出口海外仓业务退税操作指引，进一步指导企业用好现行政策。四是支持本市物流企业结合跨境电商行业发展特点，加强海运、空运、铁路、多式联运等运输保障能力建设。五是鼓励拼多多等本区跨境电商头部企业与极兔、菜鸟、货拉拉等国内物流出海头部企业开展密切合作，破解物流运输通道瓶颈。

3、持之以恒强化开放，提升跨境电商外贸采购便利化

一是建议上海向国家财政部争取自主调整优化跨境电子商务零售进口商品清单试点。在广泛征求上海各大跨境电商平台企业意见和建议的基础上，结合平台销售大数据信息，适当扩展正面清单产品目录，将OTC药品和医疗器械等国内需求旺盛产品纳入正面清单当中，丰富上海产品服务多样化供给水平。二是建议上海在无法扩展进口商品“双边互认”的前提下，进一步优化有机产品等入境后的检验检疫和市场监管流程，降低企业监管认证资金成本和时间成本，为提升进口贸易便利化水平扫除内部障碍。三是瞄准医疗消费前瞻布局新板块，推动境外消费回流。充分发挥上海生物医药产业优势，加快医疗消费品进口便利化改革进程，提高进口免税政策覆盖面。此外，加快对癌症早筛赛道的抢占和布局，抢抓医疗消费未来风口，满足上海市民对健康的多元化需求，推动境外医疗消费回流上海，打造全新消费增长点。

4、搭建业务对接平台，撬动贸易企业跨境出海积极性

整合本市2B端传统贸易商以及联合动员上海周边省市重点加工贸易企业，联动拼多多、飞书深诺、跨境共服等跨境电商平台企业，一是通过搭建业务对接服务平台，鼓励上述2B端传统贸易企业启动跨境电商业务。二是通过建立传统贸易企业数字化转型探索年度“白名单”制度，对在政府引导下首次尝试跨境电商业务而入选白名单的传统贸易企业（2B和2C均可），配套相应财政资金支持政策，以此撬动传统贸易企业开展跨境电商业务的积极性。进而以需求侧积极性激发，强化拼多多等本区龙头型跨境电商平台企业赋能引领功能，不断提升上海对长三角乃至全国地区开放型经济的赋能度和辐射力。

5、建设跨境电商创新促进平台，完善出海公共服务体系

一是针对跨境电商业务重点行业领域，分行业设立上海跨境电商（或数字贸易）创新促进平台。配套数字贸易创新促进专项资金，创新运作机制，联合行业龙头企业，发挥其辐射带动效应，打通产业链上下游，打造创新型跨境电商的协同创新载体。二是建立包括跨境电商业务在内的数字贸易知识产权管理平台，针对跨境电商乃至数字贸易各类业务场景，基于区块链等技术，建立数字知识产权全生命周期综合服务平台。建立数字贸易跨境支付结算平台，与银联、蚂蚁金服等企业开展合作，基于区块链技术，开展跨境支付结算平台建设，提高跨境支付结算效率和便利度。三是搭建企业跨境出海综合服务平台。汇聚海外各国政策法规、市场规模、竞争格局等各类数据，形成数字贸易公共数据库，助力企业开展跨境数字贸易。开展跨境贸易数据合规企业咨询服务，依托专业咨询公司等，为出口企业提供各国数据监管法规解读和合规指导，降低企业出海风险。在平台引入上海市贸促会、上海国际商会丰富的海外资源，实现国际合作资源互通共享，相互赋能，共同打造国际合作的“中转站”、国际客商的“会客厅”。

Cross-border e-commerce has emerged as a new engine for foreign trade growth and industrial expansion abroad. It is also a key channel through which Shanghai integrates into global industrial systems and strengthens the momentum of opening up. Yet in translating the empowering potential of cross-border e-commerce platforms into a higher-level open economy, Shanghai still faces several bottlenecks: the overall scale and sophistication of the cross-border e-commerce sector remain limited; air cargo capacity is approaching its ceiling; trade procurement and import facilitation on platforms fall short; and traditional trading firms show insufficient enthusiasm for entering cross-border e-commerce. Building on a systematic analysis of these constraints, this brief puts forward policy recommendations to help Shanghai break through development bottlenecks and promote high-quality growth of its open economy.

I. Key Challenges

1. Insufficient clustering of cross-border e-commerce firms, with a widening gap vis-à-vis Shenzhen

Thanks to its port advantages, Shanghai has become one of China’s major hubs for cross-border e-commerce platforms, supported by a relatively complete cross-border financial services system. Overall, it ranks among the national first tier. However, as of July 2024, Shanghai hosted 2,161 cross-border e-commerce enterprises, far fewer than Shenzhen’s 6,983. Shanghai had 611 cross-border e-commerce trading platforms, compared with Shenzhen’s 2,402.

In 2023, Shanghai’s cross-border e-commerce import and export volume reached RMB 262.39 billion, up 42 percent year on year. Shenzhen’s figure stood at RMB 3,226.53 billion, with a growth rate as high as 74.4 percent. In terms of both the number of enterprises and total transaction volume, the gap between Shanghai and Shenzhen is not only significant but continues to widen.

2. Air cargo capacity hitting a ceiling, with overseas warehouse development lagging behind

In 2023, Shanghai’s airport ports handled 440 million declared cross-border e-commerce export parcels, nearly doubling year on year and reaching a historical high, with total export value exceeding RMB 85 billion. Air cargo capacity at Shanghai’s airports is now close to its upper limit.

Executives from Pinduoduo, Shanghai’s leading local cross-border e-commerce platform, noted that cargo capacity at Hongqiao Airport can no longer keep pace with the platform’s business growth. How to open up more air freight channels, and how to better leverage sea–air coordination and multimodal transport to ease capacity constraints, has become a pressing issue that enterprises and government must address together.

3. Institutional barriers in trade procurement and import facilitation

Several institutional constraints remain. First, the current Cross-Border E-Commerce Retail Import Product List is relatively narrow. Certain frozen foods and pre-prepared meals are excluded from the positive list, limiting platforms such as Hema from fully leveraging their traffic scale and undermining efforts to integrate domestic and foreign trade.

Second, in some key sectors, regulatory requirements are overly rigid and approval processes misaligned with trade realities. For example, micro medical devices—one of the most important categories showcased at the China International Import Expo—face lengthy registration procedures when transitioning from “exhibits” to “products,” calling for urgent streamlining.

Third, fashion and consumer goods are highly time-sensitive, placing higher demands on import facilitation. Under general trade clearance, cosmetics require functional certification and testing that can take one to two years, while trend-driven “popular colors” are often seasonal. This makes it difficult for global brands to launch new products simultaneously in China and overseas. In addition, the current cap of RMB 5,000 per transaction for cross-border retail imports limits large-scale import and sales operations.

4. Limited motivation among traditional trading firms to go cross-border

Field visits to cross-border public service platforms and companies such as Pinduoduo show that many traditional trading firms in Shanghai remain cautious. Concerns over capital costs and market risks lead them to take a wait-and-see attitude toward using cross-border e-commerce platforms to expand overseas.

Those that have ventured into cross-border e-commerce often encounter challenges including overseas intellectual property disputes, unfamiliarity with foreign regulations and consumer cultures, and difficulties in building payment, settlement, and after-sales service systems. These hurdles dampen enthusiasm for further expansion. Platform operators note that most participating firms focus on B2C business, while B2B cross-border e-commerce—despite its vast market potential—has yet to receive sufficient attention.

II. Policy Recommendations

1. Combine attraction and cultivation to strengthen the cross-border e-commerce ecosystem

First, leverage leading platforms to drive large-scale, chain-based investment attraction. Platforms such as Pinduoduo and Ctrip should be encouraged to play their innovation-leading and ecosystem-building roles, using their network advantages to attract upstream and downstream firms to locate in Shanghai. Relevant authorities should design targeted industrial support policies and ensure adequate provision of office space, facilities, and services.

Second, government should take the lead in compiling and refining industrial chain maps for cross-border e-commerce and its sub-sectors. Based on these maps, Shanghai can precisely target Fortune Global 500 firms, China’s top enterprises, central and state-owned firms, and multinational corporations, and plan a pipeline of full-chain investment projects. A global investment partnership program should be implemented, strengthening cooperation with leading enterprises, intermediaries, industrial funds, and chambers of commerce.

Third, build a full ecosystem for the livestreaming economy to create differentiated advantages in cross-border expansion. Using the development of Shanghai’s livestreaming e-commerce bases as a starting point, firms should be guided to develop “Silk Road E-Commerce” livestreaming models and explore livestreaming as a tool to empower the real economy. Local universities can work with platforms such as Pinduoduo, Baiqiu, and Feishu Shenno to train livestream hosts, operators, short-video creators, and marketing planners from the ground up, incubating creator teams that operate from Shanghai’s major platform livestreaming centers.

2. Accelerate overseas warehouse development to strengthen logistics support

Existing funding channels should be better coordinated to support overseas warehouse operators, using service sector development funds to attract more market-based capital. Equity investment resources, such as those already available in Changning District, can be mobilized to explore industry development funds that strengthen support for overseas warehouses.

At the same time, clear guidelines should be issued as soon as possible on tax rebate procedures for export overseas warehouse business, helping firms better use existing policies. Logistics companies should be supported in upgrading capacity across sea, air, rail, and multimodal transport in line with cross-border e-commerce needs. Leading platforms like Pinduoduo should also be encouraged to deepen cooperation with outbound logistics leaders such as J&T Express, Cainiao, and Huolala to ease transport bottlenecks.

3. Sustain high-level opening to improve trade procurement and import facilitation

Shanghai should seek pilot authorization from the Ministry of Finance to independently adjust and optimize the retail import product list for cross-border e-commerce. Drawing on platform feedback and big data on sales, the positive list can be moderately expanded to include products in strong domestic demand, such as OTC drugs and medical devices, enriching supply diversity.

Where mutual recognition of import products cannot be expanded, inspection, quarantine, and market supervision procedures for items such as organic products should be further streamlined after entry, reducing certification costs in both time and money.

Shanghai should also look ahead in medical consumption, leveraging its strengths in biomedicine to accelerate reforms in import facilitation for medical products and expand the coverage of duty-free policies. By moving early in areas such as cancer early screening, the city can meet diversified health needs, attract overseas medical consumption back to Shanghai, and cultivate new growth drivers.

4. Build business matchmaking platforms to activate traditional traders

By integrating local B2B traditional traders and mobilizing key processing trade firms in neighboring provinces, Shanghai can work with platforms such as Pinduoduo, Feishu Shenno, and cross-border public service providers to build business matchmaking platforms that encourage traditional firms to enter cross-border e-commerce.

An annual “whitelist” system for digital transformation exploration can be introduced, offering fiscal support to traditional trading firms—both B2B and B2C—that attempt cross-border e-commerce for the first time under government guidance. By stimulating demand-side participation, Shanghai can strengthen the empowering role of leading platforms and enhance its capacity to support and radiate open economic development across the Yangtze River Delta and beyond.

5. Build innovation promotion platforms and improve public services for going global

Sector-specific innovation promotion platforms for cross-border e-commerce and digital trade should be established, supported by dedicated funds and operated through innovative mechanisms in partnership with industry leaders. These platforms can connect upstream and downstream players and serve as collaborative innovation carriers.

A digital trade intellectual property management platform should be created, covering cross-border e-commerce and related scenarios. Using technologies such as blockchain, it can provide full lifecycle IP services. A cross-border payment and settlement platform should also be developed in cooperation with firms like UnionPay and Ant Group to improve efficiency and convenience.

Finally, a comprehensive service platform for enterprises going global should be built, aggregating data on overseas regulations, market size, and competitive landscapes into a public digital trade database. Compliance consulting services can help firms navigate data regulations abroad and reduce risk. By integrating the overseas networks of the Shanghai Council for the Promotion of International Trade and the Shanghai Chamber of International Commerce, the platform can function as a hub for international cooperation—a “transfer station” for partnerships and a “meeting room” for global business.
